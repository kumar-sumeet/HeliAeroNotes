Advancing Blade Concept (ABC)¶

Schematic of lift variation over a single rotor disk in forward flight [source]

The limitations in thrust-producing abilities of the rotor on the retreating sides, due to low dynamic pressure, are a significant impediment to exploiting the full aerodynamic lifting characteristics of the blades. This is apparent from the figure above.

Schematic of thrust generated by coaxial rotor in forward flight [source]

Physical attributes¶

The advancing blade tips would start experiencing high drag as Mach divergence number (~0.9) is achieved. So to fully exploit the advantages of ABC, the rotors need to be slowed down. In the original ABC concept demonstrator (XH-49A) the tip speed was reduced from 198 m/s to 137 m/s

rotors are rigidly attached to the hub (no hinges or flexbeams!)¶
